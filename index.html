About Me


Professor Zev Ardon is the head of the Wizardry Department at Ben Gurion University (BGU), known for his brilliance and eccentricity in the magical community. Born in Tel Aviv, Zev's early fascination with the arcane arts began as a child, inspired by his grandfather, a renowned Kabbalist and scholar of ancient magical texts. His grandfather's teachings on the balance between knowledge and power shaped Zev’s view of magic as a force to be respected and carefully wielded. After studying at the prestigious Arcanum Institute in Prague, he returned to Israel, eager to blend his love for ancient magic with modern advancements in magical theory and technology.

As a professor, Ardon is known for his unorthodox teaching methods. Preferring hands-on experimentation over traditional lectures, he encourages his students to learn through practice and observation. His research in elemental transfiguration has earned him recognition worldwide, particularly for his work on integrating magic with environmental conservation efforts. Zev is also passionate about the ethical use of magic, believing that all magic must be used responsibly. This philosophy has influenced not only his academic work but also his personal life, as he often lectures on the moral dimensions of magic in both the classroom and at international magical conferences.

Outside of his academic duties, Ardon has a variety of personal interests. He is an avid collector of ancient magical artifacts and enjoys restoring them in his free time. His workshop, located in the depths of the BGU campus, is filled with magical relics and enchanted objects, many of which have been lost to time. When he’s not immersed in research or artifact restoration, Zev enjoys hiking through Israel’s rugged landscapes, where he finds peace and inspiration in nature. These outdoor excursions have also led him to study magical flora and fauna, another field of interest that occasionally overlaps with his academic research.

Ardon’s life and career have been influenced by a few key figures, but perhaps the most significant was his mentor, Professor Eliezer Goldstein, a former head of the Department of Arcane Sciences at the University of Prague. Goldstein, a legendary figure in magical research, taught Ardon the importance of humility in the pursuit of knowledge and instilled in him a lifelong respect for the complexities of magical history. Professor Goldstein’s mentorship had a profound impact on Ardon’s philosophy and approach to teaching, and the two often collaborate on research projects despite their differing locations.

Known for his calm demeanor and sharp intellect, Zev Ardon continues to shape the future of magical education at BGU, leaving an indelible mark on the magical community. His students admire his approachability and dedication, and his colleagues respect his vast knowledge and unwavering commitment to the ethical practice of magic. Ardon’s legacy, both as a teacher and a researcher, is one that will influence generations of magical practitioners to come.
